THE BROTHERS GRIMM: Nationalism in a Romantic World

STEPHANIE VU | GMU English Honors Program

Conclusion

Although there are convincing arguments that support the claim that Brothers Grimm’s works in preserving German lore were inspired by the trends of Romanticism, there are stronger arguments that the brothers actually were just as motivated in creating a German identity that would prevent unification from being threatened by forces like Napoleon. Specifically, they were biased towards bourgeois values to increase their appeal. Moreover, the brothers’ credits deliberately discredited women who contributed to their folktales in order to promote the middle-class, patriarchal German identity. Additionally, the brothers meticulously manipulated dark tales into purified children’s stories that are popular worldwide. The after-effects of success from such actions are to be explored, as it is this uniquely paradoxical sanitization and preservation of the original tales that boosted the brothers into the international spotlight.
